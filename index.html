Nürburgring Nordschleife


20.832km long, 73 corners, over 300m of elevation difference, and constantly changing road surfaces. First opened in 1927, this beautifully situated track is one of the world's most famous racing venues. With tricky corners, treacherous crests, and slopes and gradients of up to 18°, It has everything needed to test both man and machine to their limits. It is the longest permanent racetrack in the world, and, some would say, the most notorious. No wonder that legendary British racing driver Sir Jackie Stewart's nickname for the track is still used today.
